Analysis of Student English Test Scores (2024)

Systematic Approach to Data Analysis (SPSS)

The process began by loading the dataset, 'Analysing_data.sav', and defining key variables. The raw test scores (English2024) were prepared for transformation, and a new variable, English_Grades, was given the label 'English test scores'.

VARIABLE LABELS English_Grades 'English test scores'.

2. Data Cleaning and Transformation âœ¨

The variable **English2024** was **transformed** (recoded) into the categorical **English_Grades** using the `RECODE` command, grouping scores into distinct letter grades (A, B, C, D). Data quality was high, as initial analysis showed **N=20 Valid** cases and **0 Missing** values for the core variables.

RECODE English2024 (80 thru 100=1) (70 thru 79=2) (...) INTO English_Grades.

3. Data Analysis and Visualization ðŸ”¬

Multiple statistical analyses were performed using the SPSS commands:

  • Frequencies and Visualization: Used to show the distribution of **English_Grades** (e.g., 40.0% of students achieved an 'A' grade), visually represented by a Bar Chart (`GGRAPH`).
  • Correlation: A Pearson Correlation (`CORRELATIONS`) showed a **strong positive relationship** between students' **Confidence\_level** and their **English2024** score (*r* = .771, *p* < .001).
  • Partial Correlation: Examined the relationship between Total Class Attendance and English2024, controlling for Confidence\_level.
  • Independent Samples t-Test: Compared mean **English2024** scores by **Gender**, finding a **significant difference** (*t*(18) = -2.592, *p* = .018), with females scoring higher (Mean=79.30) than males (Mean=67.80).
  • Paired Samples t-Test: Compared **English2024** to **English2023** scores, showing a highly **significant mean improvement** of 40.40 points (*t*(19) = 17.545, *p* < .001).

4. Summary of Key Findings ðŸ“ˆ

The primary results indicate: 1) Student confidence is strongly linked to performance. 2) There is a significant, positive change in test scores from 2023 to 2024. 3) A notable gender gap exists, with female students outperforming male students in the 2024 test.
